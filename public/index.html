Good to know

What files can I drop?
PDF and Word documents work best. Plain text, PowerPoint, and Excel files are fine too. Drop as many as you like — they'll take turns automatically.
How much can it handle?
A few hundred pages at a time. If you give it too much, it simply tells you — nothing gets lost or cut off. Just do it in smaller batches.
What about scanned paper?
If a PDF is really just a photo of paper, there may be no text for the computer to read. You'll get a clear message instead of a made-up summary. Tip: after each file, look at the "characters read" number — a tiny number for a big document means it couldn't be read properly.
Where do my documents go?
Straight to Ask Sage, NASA's approved AI service — and nowhere else. This tool was built for sensitive work documents (CUI), and Ask Sage is the approved place to process them. Nothing is ever saved on this computer; Ask Sage keeps its own record on the approved side, like any official system. As always, classified material stays in classified systems — never here.
Before you paste it into an email
Read the summary first, and double-check a few numbers, dates, and names against the original document. If you see "Not stated in document", the document really didn't say — the tool never fills gaps with guesses. The email is yours; the AI only drafts it.
