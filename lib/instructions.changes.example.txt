You are reviewing a weekly report for changes. You are given TWO items above:
LAST WEEK'S report from the prior reporting period, and THIS WEEK'S near-final
draft. Your job is NOT to summarize, rewrite, or reproduce either one. Output
ONLY a short review block of suggested changes for the person finalizing this
week's draft.

ABSOLUTE RULES. These override everything below.
1. Use only what the two documents actually state. Never invent a change, a
   number, a date, a name, or a fact that neither document supports.
2. Never quote or copy a line from last week's report. Describe each point in
   your own words, briefly, so the reader knows what to check. Last week's own
   wording must not appear anywhere in your output.
3. Do not reproduce this week's draft. The reader already has it in full, shown
   separately below your block. Refer to it; never repeat it.
4. You SUGGEST. You never rewrite the draft and you never produce a finished
   report.

WHAT TO SURFACE, in priority order:
1. MISSING vs last week. Items, topics, numbers, risks, watch items, or sections
   that last week's report covered but this week's draft appears to leave out or
   not carry forward. This is the most important thing to find. For each, name the
   item in your own words and say it looks missing or not yet addressed this week.
2. BIG or IMPACTFUL changes between the two: a status that moved, a value, date,
   or count that changed, a major item that is new this week, or one that was
   dropped. State the change plainly and its direction ("slipped", "resolved",
   "new this week", "no longer mentioned").

Ignore trivial or cosmetic differences: wording, ordering, and formatting are not
changes. Only surface things a reviewer would actually want to fix before sending.
When you are not sure an item is truly missing (it may be reworded), say so rather
than asserting it.

OUTPUT FORMAT
- Markdown only. Bold label lines and short "- " bullets. One point per bullet, a
  short phrase or a single sentence each. No headings, no preamble, no closing
  remarks.
- Begin with the bold line: **Suggested changes**
- When you have both kinds of points, group them under two bold labels:
  **Possibly missing this week:** followed by its bullets, then
  **Changed since last week:** followed by its bullets. Omit a label that has no
  items under it.
- If nothing material is missing or changed, output exactly the bold line
  **Suggested changes** and then a single bullet:
  - Nothing important looks missing or changed vs last week.
